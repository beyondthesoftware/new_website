Why This Sprint Matters

Most dashboards inside Aspire don't fail because of the software. They fail because they're disconnected from real responsibility. When a dashboard reflects what you actually manage—and when the data behind it is trusted—it becomes more than a report. It becomes a control panel for decisions.

This sprint exists to turn dashboards from passive screens into active tools. By the end, you'll have a dashboard tied directly to your role, your goals, and a process for improving a real metric during this sprint—not someday, but now.

Dashboards are often the first place teams realize whether their systems are actually supporting the year ahead.

Who This Sprint Is For

This sprint is designed for professionals who want more than surface-level reporting. If you want dashboards that reflect real work and lead to real conversations, you're in the right place.

People Who Are Accountable for Results

  • Operations, production, maintenance, or branch managers
  • Anyone responsible for revenue, hours, routing, work quality, or job performance
  • Leaders who want to see whether goals are actually being met

People Who Own or Influence Data

  • Admins who maintain lists, timesheets, or invoicing
  • Schedulers, payroll, and billing roles
  • Anyone who touches inputs that determine accuracy

People Ready to Move From Reporting to Action

  • You've built dashboards before, but they didn't change behavior
  • You want clarity on how to use dashboards, not just build them
  • You want accountability and structure so progress actually happens

What You'll Get

You'll walk away with:

  • A dashboard built around your actual responsibilities
  • Three widgets tied directly to the metrics that matter to your role
  • A framework for evaluating accuracy and fixing "garbage in"
  • A four-week challenge that turns numbers into action, without overwhelming your week
  • SOPs, worksheets, and templates you can repeat with future metrics
  • Written feedback and a 1:1 session to personalize your approach

This isn't a passive training. It's a working sprint. You will build, analyze, troubleshoot, and improve—not just learn about dashboards.

Week-by-Week Breakdown

Week 1: Build the Dashboard

Create a dashboard aligned with three responsibilities from your intake form. Learn how to validate widget results and trace data sources.

Week 2: Own Accurate Data

Map the flow of data, identify inputs, and develop an action plan. Pick a widget for your challenge and define how you'll influence it.

Week 3: Run the Challenge

Take action on your chosen metric. Track results daily, problem-solve, and prepare for a 1:1 call for tailored guidance.

Week 4: Make It Sustainable

Write SOPs, share widgets with teammates, and establish weekly review habits. Build a roadmap for your next dashboard or teammate support.

Follow-Up Week

Receive individualized feedback and a certificate when you reach the goal you set.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much time should I expect to spend each week?

Most participants spend 2–4 hours weekly, including calls and homework.

Are the group calls required?

Not required, but highly recommended. Recordings are available.

What if my data is inaccurate?

That's expected. Week 2 is dedicated to understanding and improving accuracy.

Do we only build one dashboard?

You'll build one dashboard with three widgets and learn how to replicate the process you can use again.

Will you tell me exactly which widgets to build?

No, and that's intentional. You'll choose based on your role and goals. We guide without prescribing.

Will I really see results in just a month?

Most participants do—sometimes incremental, sometimes significant.

What kind of feedback do I get?

Written feedback on worksheets, plus a 1:1 call for personalized support.

Do you work with my teammates too?

Not during this sprint, though you'll be equipped to engage them afterward.
